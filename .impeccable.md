## Design Context

### Users

Curious readers, writers, and visual tinkerers who want to turn long-form text into a surprising piece of art. They may arrive from daviseford.com on a phone for inspiration, browse the community gallery, or use a desktop to paste a book or article, tune a palette, and download a finished SVG or PNG.

### Brand Personality

Inventive, thoughtful, and credible. Word Art should share the calm confidence and plainspoken character of Davis E. Ford's blog and landing page while feeling more playful: a small, well-made creative instrument rather than a corporate design tool.

### Aesthetic Direction

A refined editorial studio on a lightly blue-tinted paper background. Reuse the landing page's deep navy ink, blue accent, clear typographic hierarchy, crisp rules, and restrained motion. Keep the surrounding interface quiet so submitted artwork and color palettes provide the visual energy. Avoid generic SaaS dashboards, neon developer styling, busy Bootstrap grids, and decorative effects that compete with the art.

### Design Principles

1. Let the art carry the color; keep the interface calm, legible, and consistent with daviseford.com.
2. Make the first viewport explain the idea and offer a clear path to create or browse.
3. Start with text and a palette, then progressively disclose advanced color and highlight controls.
4. Treat speed and accessibility as design materials: paginated media, contained images, semantic HTML, visible focus, 44px targets, and reduced-motion support.
5. Preserve the project's handmade personality through concise, warm copy and purposeful details instead of template-like decoration.
